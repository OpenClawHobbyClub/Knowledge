# GreenTrail — Problem Brief

_Created: 2026-04-08 | Author: Wilson (PM)_

## Problem Statement

European SMB logistics companies (freight forwarders, 3PLs, regional carriers) face mandatory carbon reporting requirements under CSRD by 2027, but lack affordable, integrated tools to track and report the carbon footprint of individual shipments.

## Who Has This Problem?

**Primary:** European SMB freight forwarders and 3PLs (50–500 employees)
- Currently using spreadsheets or nothing for carbon tracking
- Can't afford enterprise sustainability platforms (Ecoinvent, Sphera)
- Face regulatory deadlines they're not equipped to meet

**Secondary:** Their customers (shippers/brands) who need Scope 3 data from logistics providers

## Why It Hurts

1. **Regulatory pressure** — CSRD 2027 creates a hard compliance deadline; non-compliance = fines and audit risk
2. **Customer pressure** — Large shippers increasingly require carbon data from logistics partners
3. **Competitive disadvantage** — Can't win RFPs that require sustainability reporting
4. **Operational blindness** — No visibility into which routes, modes, or carriers are carbon-efficient

## Why Now?

- CSRD 2027 deadline creates an urgent buying trigger (companies need to report on 2026 data)
- The airline carbon offset model has normalized per-transaction carbon visibility for consumers — logistics is next
- Tracking data already exists in TMS/ERP systems — this is an *integration play*, not a data collection problem
- EU Green Deal and Fit for 55 package are tightening the regulatory environment every year

## Key Insight

The data needed to calculate shipment-level carbon footprint *already exists* — it's in TMS systems, carrier APIs, and route planning tools. The gap isn't data collection, it's data *integration and calculation*. This makes the problem solvable without requiring customers to change their operations.
